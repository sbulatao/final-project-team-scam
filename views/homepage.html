Team: Sarah Reine Bulatao, Thao Chung (Chloe), Afraz Hameed, Minh Pham

ECE 196 FALL 2024

Short Description of Project

Problem Definition

Stormwater and Sewage Spills contain untreated debris directly into the Tijuana River and Imperial Beach affecting pH of the waters affecting marine life and making it more acidic. There are two issue that affects beach closure based on water quality which are stormwater and the sewage.The stormwater carries pollutants such as oils, chemicals, pesticides, and trash into the ocean. The Tijuana River carries untreated sewage, industrial waste, and trash flow into the river, specifically impacting the Imperial Beach. Which damages both local communities and the marine life ecosystem. The health risks are associated with contaminated seafood, recreational water exposure, and waterborne diseases to Aerosolized contaminated air. However, we will focus on the damages that are happening within the marine life ecosystem. The damages that the ecosystem are facing is that it is affecting the reproductive success of species, destabilized food webs, and degrade overall health of reefs, seagrass beds, other habitats. For this project, we will specifically put our attention of how the pH level of the waters are affecting marine life.

Solution

Our proposed solution is to have a REAL-TIME water pH Regulation Network. The pH sensor detects a body of water and regards to whether the water is acidic/alkaline, we then pump in basic solutions of either acidic/alkaline in untreated water to regulate pH level for marine life.

Testable Hypothesis

If there is polluted water with a more acidic pH, then our device will read the values and add basic solution until the pH is regular/safe for marine life.
